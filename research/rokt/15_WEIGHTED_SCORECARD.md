## Confidence ratings — TOP 8

Scale: High / Med / Low. Evidence = strength of documented pain; Novelty = distance from public Rokt/competitor capability; Build = feasibility of a convincing synthetic-data prototype; Overall = holistic proof-of-work strength.

### 1. C51 — Fail-closed placement resilience (89.5)
- **Evidence: High** — the cardinal guardrail is explicit in Rokt docs (protect checkout, fail closed) and the `PLACEMENT_FAILURE` path is public.
- **Novelty: Med** — Rokt certainly does this internally; the prototype's novelty is making non-interference *visible, measured, and audited*, not inventing it.
- **Build: High** — synthetic failure injection + `Selection.on()`/Cart API timeout is directly demoable; Pavan's circuit-breaker/fail-closed patterns transfer.
- **Overall: High** — hits the cardinal rule and the top hiring signal (failure→recovery→audit) simultaneously.

### 2. C54 — Idempotent revenue-share reconciliation ledger (88.0)
- **Evidence: High** — money integrity + at-least-once delivery are unambiguous; report 12 names this the single highest-value pattern for a Rokt build.
- **Novelty: Med** — reconciliation is internal to any ad network; the tamper-evident, verifiable split ledger is the differentiator vs Rebuy's outsourced model.
- **Build: High** — synthetic revenue/refund/duplicate streams demo cleanly; strongest Pavan fit.
- **Overall: High** — most authentic fit + strongest evidence; main risk is backend-only storytelling (pair with a ledger/reconciliation UI).

### 3. C45 — Decision-provenance spine, tamper-evident (87.8)
- **Evidence: Med-High** — automated-decisioning opt-out/explainability basis is real (GDPR/CPRA); "reconstructable money" is inferred but strong.
- **Novelty: Med-High** — no competitor exposes a tamper-evident per-decision "why this offer" spine.
- **Build: High** — hash-chain/HMAC + `mentionsWrongOffer`-style guardrail are proven Pavan patterns; synthetic decisions suffice.
- **Overall: High** — textbook responsible-AI (deterministic decides, LLM only explains); must avoid reading as a "generic explainability dashboard."

### 4. C33 — Verifiable consent + DSR receipts (81.0)
- **Evidence: High** — consent enforcement and DSR windows are legally grounded and Rokt-documented.
- **Novelty: Med-High** — cryptographic *proof of enforcement* at the offer moment is new; OneTrust manages consent but doesn't prove offer-time non-use.
- **Build: Med-High** — synthetic consent/DSR streams demo well; the "blocked reuse of deleted data" moment is dramatic.
- **Overall: High** — maximal privacy/trust wedge; risk is scope creep across the whole consent stack.

### 5. C52 — Reservation lifecycle integrity, Cart API (80.9)
- **Evidence: High** — Cart API reserve/release/confirm/cancel is fully public with an explicit auto-release-on-timeout path.
- **Novelty: Med** — order state machines are common; the Rokt-Cart-API-specific idempotent replay/recovery is the wedge.
- **Build: High** — maps directly to a public transactional surface; Pavan's Dreamship replay orchestrator transfers 1:1.
- **Overall: High** — concrete, real-surface, and Pavan-signature; slightly narrower business story than C51/C54.

### 6. C42 — Off-policy evaluation safe-rollout gate (80.8)
- **Evidence: High** — the offline↔online gap is *the* recurring RecSys pitfall; well-sourced.
- **Novelty: Med-High** — turnkey OPE gates on the *offer policy* (not just A/B stats) are Rokt-distinctive vs Statsig/Eppo.
- **Build: High** — Open Bandit Pipeline provides synthetic + ZOZO logs and the estimators; very feasible.
- **Overall: Med-High** — strong "measurement-first" alignment; demo needs a crisp visual to land the divide-by-zero/insufficient-support story in 2 min. Lower Pavan fit.

### 7. C05 — Deterministic feasible-set builder (80.6)
- **Evidence: High** — hard constraints must be deterministic/auditable (well-established; Rokt controls are public).
- **Novelty: Med** — every network filters; the auditable, testable, single-enforcement-point feasible-set trace is the differentiator.
- **Build: High** — synthetic constraints + greedy/solver are very demoable; Pavan's reconciliation single-enforcement-point transfers.
- **Overall: Med-High** — the correct-not-fallback deterministic story; unglamorous, so best paired with a ranker (C47/C06) to show the full stack.

### 8. C17 — Native incrementality / geo-lift (79.7)
- **Evidence: High** — attribution/overlap anxiety is the single most-repeated buyer pain across case studies (Cozy Earth hired Haus).
- **Novelty: Med** — incrementality vendors exist; native-to-the-offer-network measurement is the wedge and matches Rokt's public "measurement-first" push.
- **Build: High** — synthetic DGP with a known-truth effect + DoWhy refutations is clean and honest.
- **Overall: Med-High** — strategically core, but it's a measurement product (no consumer surface) and has the lowest Pavan-fit of the top 8.

## Underrated / overrated calls (honest)

**Underrated (score understates their strategic value):**
- **C17 — Native incrementality (rank 8).** Scored mid-70s but is arguably the most *strategically* important idea in the set: the single most-repeated buyer pain and a direct match to Rokt's public "measurement-first" positioning. Its low Pavan-fit and lack of a consumer surface suppress the weighted total, but as a business bet it punches above rank 8.
- **C42 — OPE safe-rollout gate (rank 6).** Undersold by demo-clarity and Pavan-fit penalties, yet it is the most intellectually honest expression of the entire report-09 thesis ("classical decision-science beats LLMs; de-risk before you ship") and is highly credible on public Open-Bandit data.

**Overrated (score flatters them; treat with caution):**
- **C04 — Warm-start CDP-conditioned decisioning (rank 10).** Rides the merger thesis to a top-15 total, but it is the closest candidate to the *rejected* "better Rokt Brain"/"generic recommendation engine" direction. Its ND sub-score (5) already reflects this; if built, it must foreground the orchestration seam + latency budget + fallback, never ranker quality — otherwise it collapses into a rejected concept.
- **C47 — Contextual-bandit cold-start (rank 25).** Demos beautifully (regret curves) which inflates DC/PF, but a bandit on its own is the textbook "generic recommendation engine." It only earns its place as a *component* inside the audited feasible-set + fail-closed + OPE envelope (C05+C51+C42+C45), not as a standalone thesis.

**Cross-cutting recommendation:** the top of the table is not eight separate products — it is one coherent flagship. **C51 + C54 + C45 + C05 + C42** compose a single "confirmation-moment decision-and-money-integrity layer" (deterministic feasible set → calibrated/bandit rank → OPE-gated rollout → fail-closed serving → tamper-evident provenance + idempotent revenue reconciliation), with C33 as the privacy wedge and LLM confined to the periphery (C60/C61). That bundle maximizes evidence, alignment, engineering depth, responsible-AI fit, and Pavan's authentic failure→recovery→audit DNA on entirely synthetic/public data.
