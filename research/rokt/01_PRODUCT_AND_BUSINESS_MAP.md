## 1. Executive summary

Rokt is an AI-powered ecommerce technology company that monetizes and optimizes the **Transaction Moment™** — the high-intent window spanning **Selection → Cart/Review → Payment → Confirmation** of an online purchase. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` Rokt sits between three parties — **ecommerce merchants/partners** (who own the checkout surface), **advertisers/brands** (who want access to high-intent shoppers), and **end customers** — and runs a "win-win-win" two-sided network powered by a proprietary AI engine (**Rokt Brain**) operating over a scaled **Rokt Network**. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`

Headline scale (from rokt.com, retrieved 2026-07-18): **"10B+ transactions annually,"** **"33,000+ clients,"** **"1.1B customers"** globally, clients in **"17 countries,"** and a Catalog of **"1.2 million third-party products from 4,600+ premium brands."** `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`

The product portfolio maps cleanly onto the four Transaction-Moment stages, plus two horizontal layers (the AI/network engine, and a customer-data platform acquired via the **mParticle** merger). Fastest-visible expansion appears to be **Rokt Catalog / Catalog for Brands** (distributed commerce), **Rokt Pay+** (payments monetization), and the **mParticle "Hybrid CDP"** data layer. `[INFERENCE]`

## 2. The Transaction Moment™ (the organizing concept)

**Definition (quoted):** the moment "from selection to confirmation, when customer attention, trust, and intent are at their peak." At this point "attention is highest, intent is clearest, and trust is strongest." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` — *What is the Transaction Moment™?*, rokt.com/blog, last updated 2025-09-11.

**The four stages / sequence** `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`:

| # | Stage | Rokt product(s) active | What happens |
|---|-------|------------------------|--------------|
| 1 | **Selection / Discovery** | Rokt Catalog | Expand assortment with curated third-party products in search/category/PDP |
| 2 | **Cart / Review** | Rokt Upcart | AI-selected in-cart upsells (first- + third-party) |
| 3 | **Payment** | Rokt Pay+ | Payment-method offers & incentives compete for "share of wallet" |
| 4 | **Confirmation / Thank-you** | Rokt Thanks; Rokt Aftersell | Post-purchase offers; one-click post-checkout upsell |

Rokt claims the Transaction Moment "drives over 90% of ancillary revenue potential." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` (rokt.com homepage)

**Where Rokt enters the journey:** Predominantly **during and after** the Transaction Moment — from cart through the post-purchase confirmation page. Discovery-stage entry (Catalog) is the earliest touch; Aftersell/Thanks are the latest (post-payment). Rokt is **not** primarily a top-of-funnel acquisition/awareness tool for the host merchant; it activates where purchase intent is already high. `[INFERENCE]`

## 3. Product portfolio (surface-by-surface)

### 3.1 Rokt Brain + Rokt Network (the engine — horizontal layer)
- **Rokt Brain:** proprietary AI engine that "processes billions of transactions instantly, connecting each customer with the most relevant products, services, or offers." Also "dynamically generate[s] creatives, formats, and layouts." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` (rokt.com/products/rokt-brain-and-rokt-network)
- **Decisioning principle:** "show the right content or show nothing" — enforces minimum quality standards on every recommendation. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` (search-surfaced product copy)
- **Training scale:** Reported variously as "more than 6 billion transactions annually" and "7.5+ billion transactions processed annually"; Aftersell page cites **"more than 1.95 trillion data points per year."** Figures differ across pages/dates — treat as order-of-magnitude. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` (figures) / `[INFERENCE]` (reconciliation)
- **Brain v4:** Rokt announced **"Brain v4"**, the latest evolution of the engine, positioned to improve real-time relevance at checkout. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` (TipRanks/PRNewswire coverage referencing Rokt announcement; secondary)
- **Rokt Network:** a **"closed ecommerce network"** of partner sites; runs on "native integrations," "network effects," and a **"win-win-win commercial model"** aligning "partners, advertisers, and customers." Each transaction improves AI performance for all participants (stated network effect). `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`

### 3.2 Rokt Catalog — *Selection/Discovery* (distributed commerce)
- **What:** AI-powered distributed-commerce platform bringing **"1.2 million products"** from **"4,600+ curated brands"** into partner sites without the partner managing inventory/logistics. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Revenue model (notable specificity):** Partner acts as **Merchant of Record** and "earn[s] commission on every transaction (typically 18–25%)"; Rokt absorbs "sourcing, compliance, returns, and vendor payouts." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Positioning:** "More GMV, less lift"; onboarding "6x faster… vs. legacy benchmarks," "90% automation." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`

### 3.3 Rokt Catalog for Brands — *supply side of Catalog*
- **Customer:** "DTC, digitally native, and established consumer brands." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **What they buy:** distribution/shoppable placements "across trusted retail channels" via a single integration.
- **Pricing:** "performance-based distribution channel, where brands pay only when a sale happens." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Value:** "up to 6× faster" onboarding, "~20 hours average work eliminated per new channel." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Interpretation:** Catalog and Catalog-for-Brands are the two sides of one marketplace — retailers buy assortment breadth; brands buy distribution reach. `[INFERENCE]`

### 3.4 Rokt Upcart — *Cart/Review*
- **What:** "AI-powered ecommerce solution that surfaces relevant first-party and third-party upsells directly on the shopping cart page." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Claim:** "over 25% average order value (AOV) lift" (case example "30% increase in incremental revenue"). `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Control:** "partners can allow, block, or prioritize specific brands." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`

### 3.5 Rokt Pay+ — *Payment*
- **What:** optimizes the payments page; "Turn a Cost Center Into a Profit Engine." Payment methods "compete for share of wallet and unlock purchase incentives." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Partners surfaced:** offers from "digital wallets, credit cards, BNPL providers, cashback programs, loyalty schemes, and gift-with-purchase partners"; **Klarna** named as a partner (Klarna Head of NA quoted on "incremental value"). `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Claims:** "~$400K in incremental profit per 1M transactions," "0 negative impact on customer conversion rates." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`

### 3.6 Rokt Aftersell — *Confirmation (SMB/Shopify)*
- **What:** post-purchase "relevant one-click upsell offers immediately after checkout" with no re-entry of payment/shipping; aimed at **SMB and Shopify merchants** (product previously known as "AfterSell," now "AfterSell powered by Rokt"). `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Claims:** "Up to 30% More Revenue From Every Customer," "$5+ to every order." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Note:** distinct SKU/domain (mainsite.rokt.com/products/aftersell-by-rokt) — appears to be Rokt's self-serve/SMB on-ramp vs. enterprise products. `[INFERENCE]`

### 3.7 Rokt Thanks — *Confirmation (enterprise)*
- **What:** activates on the confirmation page with "premium first and third-party offers"; "Show highly relevant post-purchase content" while avoiding "cluttered, low-quality ad experiences." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Advertiser example:** PayPal quoted — "surprise and delight consumers to buy when they're already in the shopping mindset." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Interpretation:** Thanks (enterprise confirmation-page monetization) and Aftersell (SMB one-click post-purchase upsell) are adjacent post-purchase products serving different segments. `[INFERENCE]`

### 3.8 Rokt Ads — *the advertiser demand side*
- **What:** lets advertisers "acquire customers while they shop and only pay for outcomes." Ads appear on "premium inventory across Rokt's closed ecommerce network" during checkout. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Advertisers named:** Ticketmaster, Domino's, Wayfair, Southwest Airlines, Uber. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Pricing:** "dynamic Cost per Click (CPC) model… optimized to meet campaign goals (CPA, ROAS, LTV)"; "closed-loop measurement and machine learning optimization." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Interpretation:** Rokt Ads is the **demand engine** that fills the third-party inventory shown inside Upcart/Thanks/Pay+/Catalog surfaces; it is what advertisers "buy" and what generates the revenue Rokt shares with merchants. `[INFERENCE]`

### 3.9 Rokt mParticle — *customer-data platform (horizontal data layer)*
- **What:** a CDP that "collects and connects customer data across digital and offline sources, unifying identities and routing insights to analytics, AI, and monetization tools"; "300+ native integrations"; "real-time identity resolution." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Acquisition:** Rokt announced a **US$300M** merger/investment in mParticle, **announced ~Jan 15–16, 2025**, committing to "double the total investment"; Rokt valuation cited at **US$3.5B**. Leadership: Michael Katz stays mParticle CEO; **Andrew Katz becomes Rokt CTO**; Jason Lynn stays mParticle CPO. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` (rokt.com/blog + PRNewswire/AdExchanger/Lowenstein secondary)
- **Data flow:** "Unknown User → Customer 360 → Enrichment → Segmentation → Rokt Brain activation"; feeds "live conversion data into ad platforms to refine targeting, bidding, and ROAS." Integrated with Brain claims "up to 30% higher value per transaction." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- **Hybrid CDP:** mParticle launched a "Hybrid CDP on Snowflake AI Data Cloud," uniting real-time activation with cloud-native/warehouse flexibility. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` (mparticle.com/news, secondary-primary)

## 4. Who pays Rokt, and what each party buys

| Party | Pays Rokt? | What they buy (outcome purchased) | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|-----------------------------------|----------|
| **Ecommerce merchant/partner** (enterprise) | Mostly **paid BY Rokt** (revenue share) | Incremental revenue/margin, AOV lift, expanded assortment, monetized checkout & confirmation — with control over experience | "$7 of every $8 in value goes to our partners"; Catalog commission "18–25%" to partner `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` |
| **SMB / Shopify merchant** (Aftersell) | Likely pays fee / rev-share | One-click post-purchase upsell revenue ("$5+ per order") | Aftersell page `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` / model `[INFERENCE]` |
| **Advertiser / brand (demand side)** | **Pays Rokt** | High-intent customer acquisition & conversions; "pay only for outcomes" (CPC→CPA/ROAS/LTV) | Rokt Ads page `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` |
| **Brand supplying Catalog** | **Pays Rokt** (only on sale) | Distribution/shoppable placement across retail network | Catalog for Brands `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` |
| **Payment partners** (Klarna, wallets, BNPL, card issuers) | **Pay Rokt** (`[INFERENCE]`) | "Share of wallet" — being the selected/incentivized payment method at checkout | Rokt Pay+ page `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` (partner list) / payment `[INFERENCE]` |
| **End customer** | Does **not** pay Rokt | Relevance: "the right offers at the right time," incentives, discounts | Transaction Moment blog `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` |

**Core money flow (synthesis):** Advertisers, Catalog brands, and payment partners pay Rokt for outcomes/placements at high-intent moments; Rokt uses the Brain/Network to place only relevant offers on merchant surfaces; Rokt shares the large majority of that value with the host merchant ("$7 of every $8"). The end customer pays nothing extra and receives relevance/incentives. `[INFERENCE]` built on `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` components.

## 5. Merchant responsibility vs. Rokt as trusted intermediary

**Stays with the merchant:**
- Ownership of the checkout surface, the customer relationship, and **first-party data** — "Clients own their data"; "brands maintain full control of their first-party data." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- Commercial control: partners can "allow, block, or prioritize specific brands"; "full transparency and control" over offers shown. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- In **Rokt Catalog**, the partner is the **Merchant of Record** for the transaction (they front the sale and earn commission). `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`

**Rokt becomes the trusted intermediary for:**
- AI relevance/decisioning (Brain), creative generation, and quality enforcement ("show the right content or show nothing"). `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- Sourcing, compliance, **returns, and vendor payouts** in Catalog ("Rokt absorbs operational costs"). `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
- Advertiser demand, closed-loop measurement, billing on outcomes, and the closed-network inventory marketplace. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]` / `[INFERENCE]`
- Security & compliance posture: "SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certified"; mParticle adds "GDPR/CCPA compliance" and "encrypted clean rooms." No reliance on third-party cookies. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`

**Interpretation:** Rokt positions itself as an *aligned* intermediary rather than a disintermediator — it explicitly keeps data ownership and experience control with the merchant while taking on the marketplace, AI, and (in Catalog) fulfillment-operations burden. `[INFERENCE]`

## 6. Customer-data flows (end-to-end)

1. **Signal capture:** Merchant provides "purchase context and first-party signals (when provided)" at the Transaction Moment; mParticle additionally ingests data "across digital and offline sources." `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
2. **Identity/enrichment:** mParticle: "Unknown User → Customer 360 → Enrichment → Segmentation" via real-time identity resolution. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
3. **Decisioning:** Rokt Brain evaluates "thousands of contextual and behavioral signals in real time" to choose the offer/creative. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
4. **Activation:** relevant first-/third-party offer rendered on the merchant surface (Upcart/Pay+/Thanks/Aftersell/Catalog). `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
5. **Measurement/feedback:** conversion data flows back (closed-loop; "live conversion data into ad platforms") and improves the Brain for all network participants (network effect). `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
6. **Privacy stance:** no third-party cookies; permissioned first-party data; clients retain ownership; SOC2/ISO/GDPR/CCPA. `[VERIFIED-PUBLIC]`
